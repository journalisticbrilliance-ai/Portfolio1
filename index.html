How I work

Operating principles.

Not a methodology. Not a framework. How I think about enterprise implementation work.

01
Process sets the foundation. Relationships are built on trust.
Strong customer relationships don’t come from being easy to work with — they come from doing what you said you would do. I lead with structure: clear scope, defined owners, and a documented path forward. When the process holds and commitments are kept, customer trust follows naturally. That trust is what makes the relationship durable.
Leadership philosophy
02
Clarity upfront protects the customer relationship later.
The most damaging thing you can do to a customer relationship is overpromise and underdeliver. Locking scope before anything moves isn’t a bureaucratic exercise — it’s how I protect the customer from disappointment. When both sides agree on what done looks like before work begins, the entire engagement runs on honesty rather than assumption.
03
Honest conversations early protect customer satisfaction later.
Customers don’t lose trust because problems exist — they lose trust because problems were hidden. I keep a live risk register from day one and surface issues early, even when it’s uncomfortable. An honest conversation in week two is how you preserve the relationship in week eight. Transparency isn’t just a process discipline — it’s the foundation of customer confidence.
Leadership philosophy
04
Speed to value is how you earn the right to the relationship.
Customer satisfaction isn’t built in kickoff meetings — it’s built when the customer can show their leadership something real. My first question in any engagement: what’s the fastest path to a tangible outcome? A meaningful win in the first two weeks changes the customer’s internal narrative and earns the credibility that makes every subsequent conversation easier.
Leadership philosophy
