How I work

Operating principles.

Not a methodology. Not a framework. How I think about enterprise implementation work.

01
Process sets the foundation. Relationships are built on trust.
Strong customer relationships don’t come from being easy to work with — they come from doing what you said you would do. I lead with structure: clear scope, defined success criteria, and transparent communication.
Leadership philosophy
02
Clarity upfront protects the customer relationship later.
The most damaging thing you can do to a customer relationship is overpromise and underdeliver. Locking scope before anything moves isn’t a bureaucratic exercise — it protects the relationship.
03
Honest conversations early protect customer satisfaction later.
Customers don’t lose trust because problems exist — they lose trust because problems were hidden. I keep a live risk register from day one and surface issues early, with options for mitigation.
Leadership philosophy
04
Speed to value is how you earn the right to the relationship.
Customer satisfaction isn’t built in kickoff meetings — it’s built when the customer can show their leadership something real. My first question in any engagement is: What can we deliver in 30 days?
Leadership philosophy
